Classic Spaghetti Carbonara - Authentic Italian Pasta Recipe

⏱️ 30 minutes | 👥 4 servings | 🔥 Medium difficulty

Spaghetti Carbonara is one of the most beloved and authentic Italian pasta dishes in the world. This creamy, delicious Roman pasta recipe has been passed down through generations and remains a favorite in restaurants and home kitchens alike. Our FoodCook version brings you the traditional preparation method that will make your taste buds dance with joy.

Ingredients for Carbonara Pasta:

  • 1 pound (500g) spaghetti pasta
  • 6 ounces (175g) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 cups freshly grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper (freshly cracked)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt for pasta water

Detailed Cooking Instructions:

Step 1: Start by bringing a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil. The water should be as salty as seawater, as this is the only opportunity to season the pasta itself. Add the spaghetti and cook according to the package directions until it reaches al dente consistency (usually 9-12 minutes). While the pasta cooks, prepare your sauce.

Step 2: In a medium bowl, whisk together the 4 large eggs with the cracked black pepper and 1 cup of grated Pecorino Romano cheese. This egg mixture creates the signature creamy sauce of carbonara. The key is not to use cream – authentic carbonara relies on the emulsification of eggs and cheese with pasta water to create a silky texture.

Step 3: In a large skillet, cook the diced pancetta or guanciale over medium heat until it becomes crispy and releases its delicious fat, approximately 5-7 minutes. Add the minced garlic in the last minute of cooking and stir until fragrant. This creates the flavorful base of your carbonara sauce.

Step 4: When the spaghetti is perfectly cooked, reserve 2 cups of the pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta. Immediately add the hot spaghetti to the skillet with the pancetta and garlic, tossing to coat with the fat.

Step 5: Remove the skillet from heat. Pour the egg mixture into the hot pasta while tossing constantly and vigorously. The residual heat will cook the eggs and create a smooth, creamy sauce. Add pasta water in small increments (about ½ cup at a time) to achieve the desired creamy consistency. The sauce should coat every strand of pasta beautifully.

Step 6: Taste and season with additional black pepper and Pecorino Romano cheese to your preference. The pasta should be creamy and well-coated with the delicious sauce. Serve immediately in warmed bowls with extra cheese and cracked black pepper on top.

Nutritional Information per Serving:

Calories: 580 | Protein: 28g | Carbs: 62g | Fat: 22g | Fiber: 2g

Chef's Tips for Perfect Carbonara:

  • Use authentic Pecorino Romano cheese, not Parmesan, for the true Roman taste
  • Never use cream in carbonara – it's not traditional and the eggs create a better sauce
  • Have all ingredients prepared before cooking the pasta
  • Work quickly once the pasta is drained to prevent the eggs from scrambling
  • The heat from the pasta cooks the eggs perfectly – never let the skillet get too hot

Creamy Chicken Tikka Masala - Authentic Indian Curry Recipe

⏱️ 45 minutes | 👥 4-6 servings | 🔥 Medium difficulty

Chicken Tikka Masala is one of the most popular and beloved Indian curry dishes served in restaurants worldwide. This rich, creamy, and aromatic dish combines tender chicken in a silky tomato and cream sauce with perfectly balanced spices. FoodCook brings you an authentic restaurant-quality recipe that you can easily prepare in your own kitchen.

Ingredients for Chicken Tikka Masala:

  • 2 pounds (1 kg) boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt (for marinating)
  • 3 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons tikka masala spice blend
  • 1 tablespoon turmeric powder
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup heavy cream or coconut milk
  • 4 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 2 large onions, finely sliced
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Detailed Cooking Instructions:

Step 1 - Marinating the Chicken: In a large bowl, combine the yogurt with 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste, 1 tablespoon tikka masala spice, and salt. Add the chicken pieces and coat thoroughly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 8 hours for deeper flavor absorption. This marination is crucial for tenderizing the chicken and infusing it with spices.

Step 2 - Cooking the Marinated Chicken: Heat 2 tablespoons of ghee in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Working in batches, place the marinated chicken pieces in the hot skillet and cook for 5-7 minutes per side until lightly browned. The chicken doesn't need to be cooked through at this stage as it will continue cooking in the sauce. Transfer the cooked chicken to a plate and set aside.

Step 3 - Preparing the Sauce Base: Add the remaining 2 tablespoons of ghee to the same skillet. Add the sliced onions and cook until they become golden brown and caramelized, approximately 8-10 minutes. This takes time but creates an essential flavor foundation for your tikka masala sauce. Stir frequently to ensure even cooking and prevent burning.

Step 4 - Building Flavor with Spices: Add the remaining 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste to the onions and cook for 2 minutes until fragrant. Add the turmeric powder, paprika, and 1 tablespoon garam masala. Stir constantly for 1-2 minutes to toast the spices and release their aromatic oils. This step is essential for authentic flavor.

Step 5 - Creating the Creamy Sauce: Add the can of crushed tomatoes to the spice mixture. Stir well to combine all ingredients. Simmer the sauce for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, to allow the tomatoes to cook down and the spices to fully incorporate. The sauce should begin to thicken slightly.

Step 6 - Finishing the Dish: Pour in the heavy cream or coconut milk slowly while stirring continuously. This creates the signature creamy texture. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet and stir to coat with the sauce. Simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and tender. The sauce should be smooth, creamy, and cling beautifully to each piece of chicken.

Step 7 - Final Touches: Taste and adjust seasonings with salt, pepper, and additional spices if needed. Garnish generously with fresh cilantro. Serve hot with basmati rice, naan bread, or your favorite Indian accompaniments.

Nutritional Information per Serving:

Calories: 520 | Protein: 45g | Carbs: 18g | Fat: 28g | Fiber: 2g

Chef's Tips for Restaurant-Quality Tikka Masala:

  • Use full-fat yogurt for better marination and creamier sauce
  • Don't skip the marinating step – it makes chicken incredibly tender
  • Ghee adds authentic Indian flavor; use it if available
  • Caramelize the onions properly for deep, rich flavor
  • Cook low and slow for best results; rushing creates watery sauce
  • Add cream at the end to maintain its creamy texture

Decadent Chocolate Lava Cake - Molten Chocolate Dessert Recipe

⏱️ 25 minutes | 👥 4 servings | 🔥 Easy difficulty

Chocolate Lava Cake is the ultimate indulgence for chocolate lovers everywhere. This elegant yet surprisingly easy dessert features a soft, fluffy chocolate cake exterior that gives way to a warm, melting chocolate center – the famous "lava" that makes this dessert so spectacular. Perfect for impressing dinner guests or treating yourself to something special.

Ingredients for Chocolate Lava Cake:

  • 4 ounces (115g) dark chocolate (70% cocoa), chopped
  • 4 tablespoons (57g) unsalted butter
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of sea salt
  • Butter and cocoa powder for ramekins
  • Powdered sugar for dusting
  • Whipped cream and fresh berries for serving (optional)

Detailed Cooking Instructions:

Step 1 - Prepare the Ramekins: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Generously butter four 6-ounce ramekins or small baking dishes. Dust the inside of each ramekin with cocoa powder, rotating to coat evenly and tapping out excess. This prevents sticking and adds extra chocolate flavor. Place the prepared ramekins on a baking sheet.

Step 2 - Melt the Chocolate and Butter: In a microwave-safe bowl, combine the chopped dark chocolate and butter. Microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring between each interval, until completely melted and smooth. Alternatively, use a double boiler method by placing a heat-safe bowl over simmering water. The chocolate should be smooth and glossy. Avoid overheating or the chocolate will become grainy.

Step 3 - Combine Eggs with Chocolate: In a separate bowl, whisk together the 2 whole eggs, 2 egg yolks, and granulated sugar. Whisk vigorously for 2-3 minutes until the mixture becomes pale, thick, and slightly ribbony. This incorporates air, giving the cakes their desired fluffy texture. The eggs are the key to achieving the perfect balance between the fluffy exterior and molten center.

Step 4 - Fold Ingredients Together: Gently fold the melted chocolate mixture into the egg mixture using a spatula, being careful not to deflate the eggs. Fold until just combined and no streaks of chocolate or egg white remain. Add the vanilla extract and sea salt, folding to incorporate evenly. The batter should be smooth but still light.

Step 5 - Add Flour: Sift the all-purpose flour over the chocolate batter and gently fold it in until just combined. Do not overmix at this stage as this can result in tough cakes. The batter should be thick but still slightly glossy.

Step 6 - Fill and Bake: Divide the batter evenly among the four prepared ramekins, filling each about three-quarters full. Place the baking sheet with ramekins in the preheated oven. Bake for 12-14 minutes. The tops should be set and slightly firm to the touch, but the centers should still feel soft and have a slight jiggle when gently shaken.

Step 7 - Serve Immediately: Remove the ramekins from the oven and let rest for 1-2 minutes. Run a thin knife around the edge of each ramekin and invert onto serving plates. The cake should slide out easily, revealing the warm molten chocolate center. Serve immediately while still warm. Dust with powdered sugar and garnish with whipped cream and fresh berries if desired.

Nutritional Information per Serving:

Calories: 380 | Protein: 7g | Carbs: 32g | Fat: 26g | Fiber: 2g

Chef's Tips for Perfect Lava Cakes:

  • Use high-quality dark chocolate for best flavor
  • Don't overbake – the centers must remain molten and warm
  • Prepare ramekins thoroughly to prevent sticking
  • Bring eggs to room temperature for best results
  • Serve immediately while the chocolate is still melted
  • Pairs beautifully with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream

Pan-Seared Lemon Garlic Salmon - Healthy Fish Dinner Recipe

⏱️ 20 minutes | 👥 2 servings | 🔥 Easy difficulty

Salmon is a nutritional powerhouse and one of the healthiest proteins you can cook at home. This pan-seared lemon garlic salmon recipe is incredibly easy to prepare, taking just 20 minutes from start to finish. The crispy skin contrasts beautifully with the tender, moist flesh inside, while the bright lemon and aromatic garlic create a delicious sauce that's simply irresistible. Perfect for weeknight dinners or entertaining guests.

Ingredients for Lemon Garlic Salmon:

  • 2 salmon fillets (6 oz each), skin-on
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, sliced thin
  • 2 fresh lemons
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Fresh thyme sprigs (or ½ teaspoon dried)
  • Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Detailed Cooking Instructions:

Step 1 - Prepare the Salmon: Remove the salmon fillets from the refrigerator 10 minutes before cooking to allow them to come closer to room temperature. This ensures even cooking throughout. Pat the fillets dry with paper towels – this is crucial for achieving a crispy skin. Generously season both sides with sea salt and freshly ground black pepper. The seasoning should be visible on the surface of the fish.

Step 2 - Heat the Skillet: Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the extra virgin olive oil and swirl to coat the pan evenly. The oil should shimmer but not smoke. If you prefer, use half butter and half oil for richer flavor, but olive oil is traditional for this preparation.

Step 3 - Sear the Salmon: Place the salmon fillets skin-side down in the hot skillet. Don't move them for 4-5 minutes – this is essential for developing a golden, crispy skin. Resist the urge to flip or fidget with the fish. You should hear a satisfying sizzle when the fish hits the pan. The salmon will naturally release from the pan when the skin is perfectly crispy.

Step 4 - Cook the Flesh Side: Carefully flip the fillets and cook the flesh side for an additional 3-4 minutes until the fish is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork. The internal temperature should reach 145°F (63°C). Don't overcook or the salmon will become dry. The flesh should still be slightly moist in the center when removed from heat.

Step 5 - Create the Lemon Garlic Sauce: While the salmon cooks on the flesh side, add the sliced garlic, butter, thyme sprigs, and juice of 1 lemon to the pan. The garlic will infuse the butter with its aromatic flavor, creating a delicious sauce. Add a pinch of red pepper flakes if you enjoy some heat. Let this simmer for 1-2 minutes.

Step 6 - Final Touches: Transfer the salmon to serving plates and spoon the warm garlic lemon butter sauce over the top. Squeeze additional fresh lemon juice over each fillet. Garnish with fresh parsley and thin lemon slices. The warm butter sauce should be drizzled around the salmon on the plate for visual appeal and extra flavor.

Nutritional Information per Serving:

Calories: 420 | Protein: 38g | Carbs: 2g | Fat: 30g | Omega-3s: 2,200mg

Chef's Tips for Perfect Salmon:

  • Buy high-quality, fresh salmon from a reliable fishmonger
  • Don't flip the salmon too early – crispy skin takes time
  • Use a meat thermometer to avoid overcooking (target 145°F)
  • Serve immediately while the skin is still crispy
  • Pairs well with roasted vegetables or a fresh salad
